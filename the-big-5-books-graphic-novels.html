Watchmen (2019 Edition)

by Alan Moore
A hit HBO original series, Watchmen, the groundbreaking series from award-winning author Alan Moore, presents a world where the mere presence of American superheroes changed history--the U.S. won the Vietnam War, Nixon is still president, and the Cold War is in full effect.
Find out more at Bookshop.org or Amazon.com

Locke & Key, Vol. 1: Welcome to Lovecraft

by Joe Hill
"In a year of auspicious comics debuts, Joe Hill turned in one of the few comics that truly came from a different place. Written with skill that makes me jealous, courage that makes me smile, and honesty and brutality that makes me want to see what comes next. Okay, I'm officially a fan." -- Ed Brubaker
Find out more at Bookshop.org or Amazon.com

The Old Guard Book One: Opening Fire

by Greg Rucka
"An action blockbuster with historical tragedy in its bones." -The AV Club"Something refreshing and new." -IGNOld soldiers never die...and some of them don't even fade away. Cursed with immortality, Andromache of Scythia and her comrades-in-arms ply their trade for those who can find-and afford-their services. But in the 21st century, being immortal is a difficult secret to keep, and when you live long enough, you learn that there are many fates far worse than death.
Find out more at Bookshop.org or Amazon.com

The Complete Persepolis

by Marjane Satrapi
"A memoir of growing up as a girl in revolutionary Iran, Persepolis provides a unique glimpse into a nearly unknown and unreachable way of life... That Satrapi chose to tell her remarkable story as a gorgeous comic book makes it totally unique and indispensable." --Time
Find out more at Bookshop.org or Amazon.com

The Complete Maus: A Survivor's Tale

by Art Spiegelman
"Spiegelman has turned the exuberant fantasy of comics inside out by giving us the most incredible fantasy in comics' history: something that actually occurred.... The central relationship is not that of cat and mouse, but that of Art and Vladek. Maus is terrifying not for its brutality, but for its tenderness and guilt."-The New Yorker
Find out more at Bookshop.org or Amazon.com
